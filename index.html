INDIVIDUAL PROJECT #1

Study Goal

How can companies increase the amount of items bought by college students on mobile devices? Students have a relatively low budget to work with as most don’t have a stable source of income or they work at relatively low paying jobs, so companies don’t want to alienate an entire potential group of customers, only because they don’t have a lot of disposable income. College students have very social lives and some put more time and effort into their social lives rather than the work they are supposed to be doing. This proposes an opportunity for students to share the latest trends in fashion, technology, etc. with each other, which could potentially lead to more sales of certain items on e-shopping sites.

Target Audience

The Target Audience of this project is college students. As stated above, college students spread trends like a plague among themselves, so a product or idea that launches in a college campus setting has the potential to gain a large customer base, very quickly. Also college students are more open to trying new things, so it will be easier to convince them to try your new product or idea.

Research Methods

I asked the questions from my survey to my participants and did not really differ from them, because I didn't feel I needed to to get the information I was looking for.

How I Recruited Users

I talked to a few of my friends in each of my classes and was able to find three of them to survey for this project.

Number of Participants

3

Key Insights From The Interviews

Mobile Shopping Is Convenient:

All three participants said that it is more convenient to buy things on their phone, rather than on their computer. Unsurprisingly, all three participants spend the majority of their money on Amazon. All three of the people I talked to had Amazon Prime Student accounts too. Relic said that he mainly browses for items on his phone and will place them in his shopping cart on his phone, but when it comes to purchasing the actual item, he will usually do it from his computer “just to make sure everything looks good”. Mac also said that “it’s hard to catch deals when you’re at your computer, vs. looking at them as they pop up on your phone.”, so this adds to the convenience factor.

Amazon Is Convenient:

Amazon offers two day shipping on all or most of the products on their store. They also have Prime Video, which is similar to Netflix and Hulu, Audiobooks and Ebooks via Audible and Kindle, a storage solution similar to Google Drive, a Credit Card with Cash Back, etc. Amazon is trying to be an all in one solution for everything on the internet and it’s working. Physical stores are closing everywhere around the country and companies that aren’t adjusting to this new e-commerce trend are going under extremely fast or are struggling i.e. Sears, Toys R’ Us, Gamestop, etc.

New Apps And New Features In Apps Have The Potential To Spread Like Wildfire:

Everyone I interviewed said that they, at least, sometimes tell their network of people about new features of apps and/or new apps, so that means, if a new app were to hit the market, it would have the potential to spread like wildfire on a college campus. This was shown when Snapchat redesigned their app and it caused a ton of people to talk about it on Facebook and Twitter and on campus too, so it’s easier than ever to release a new app or feature and have it either bring in a lot of customers or turn them away.

Amazon Provides Enough of a Value Proposition For College Students To Purchase It:

With there being an Amazon shipping store right off campus, and Amazon offering one day shipping on select products, if you ship it to that store, there is a huge incentive to buy Amazon Prime for students, because they can get anything they want delivered right to them. They don’t have to worry about finding a way to get off campus to buy items and they can always count on Amazon having the lowest prices for products.
